Nuoc Cham

Nuoc Cham aka vietnamese dipping sauce

Traditional Vietnamese Dipping sauce, not just used for dipping but can be used as a sauce to put over your favorite dish. the sweet, sour, spicy and umami profile make it perfect addition for pretty much any dish.

A staple in Vietnamese diet, it would be odd to not see a bowl of nuoc cham at a dinner table in Vietnam.

Every family has there on take on nuoc cham, some add more ingredients, some add more spice, more sour, depends on taste. When I make it i use a strict ratio, when my father makes it, he adds ingredients by taste and color

As this is a recipe I will give you the ratio that I use but not the exact amount, I believe that is part of the fun on making your own take of nuoc cham.

Ingredients

I have provided an ingredient list for my version of nuoc cham. Others may add more garlic, more or less chilli, and even add carrots, I will just give you the base you need and what I use at home

  • Water - 5 parts
  • Fish Sauce - 1 part. I use Squid brand make sure its a nice golden orange color, if it is too dark the flavor will be slightly off
  • Fresh lime juice - 1 part
  • Sugar - 1 Part
  • Garlic - to taste
  • Chilli - to taste (I use red chilli's to give the sauce a vibrant color)

The amount of chilli and garlic required depends on how much water you use, I roughly put in a tablespoon of each for every tablespoon of fish sauce I use.

Steps

  1. Get Container or bowl big enough for all your ingredients, add the water, fish sauce, and lime.
  2. Add in sugar, stir until dissolved.
  3. Chop Garlic and Chilli into small pieces and then using mortar and pestle crush.
  4. Add chilli and Garlic to the rest of the mixture.
  5. Stir, and that's it.

You can use the dipping sauce right away. To store keep in air tight container and when using make sure you use a clean plastic spoon and the sauce will stay good for up to about 2 weeks.
